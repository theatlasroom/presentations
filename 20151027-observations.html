CourtBot

  • Developed by the Atlanta CfA fellows
  • In Atlanta, when you receive a traffic infringement, no matter how minor, the simplest way to resolve it is to go to court and settle the matter. This results in people taking the day off work, only to stand in line waiting to get in.
  • So what courtbot offers, is on the spot information about traffic citations
  • Text your citation number, receive information on how to resolve your infringment

    • CourtBot can provide information on:

    • If a case can be paid immediately, the app offers a phone number and link to begin payment.
    • If a case requires a court appearance, the app allows users to sign up for reminders, served 24 hours in advance of the case.
    • If a case isn't in the system (usually because it takes two weeks for paper citations to be put into the computer), the app allows users to get information when it becomes available. The app continues checking each day for up to 16 days and sends the case information when found (or an apology if not).
    • Steps to take to challenge the citation

Promptly

Many food stamp recipients in San Francisco discover theyve been disenrolled from the program when it is too late, and they are actually at the grocery store

Recipients are then required to re-apply to be put back on the benefits program

Promptly was designed to identify recipients that were about to be disenrolled and send them a text message notification before its too late

While not strictly for food stamps, promptly provides features for scheduling, monitoring and sending text message notifications for any reason

The Hive

By: Harvest Digital Planning

The Hive is a simple set of drag and drop tools you can configure to quickly produce a website to promote a topic or drive user engagement

Some of the sites developed have been for

  • Events - communicating and promotion
  • Highlighting issues that require community feedback or participation

Out of the box, the hive offers tools like

  • Support for all types of multimedia
  • Maps for communicating spatial information, or for map based feedback
  • Infographics for communicating concepts and ideas
  • Dashboard for the clients, to allow you to keep track of consultation results and track real time website visits

Some observations...

Here are some things that i've observed in my time here that may or may not necessarily be the case

Informal knowledge sharing

  • Lots of verbal information sharing
  • Are we missing opportunities for collaboration?

A lot of knowledge seems to be shared through conversation, that's great but with staff constantly coming and going we might be missing some opportunities for collaboration

Can we improve the ways we stay informed of:

  • Other community services
  • Other courts at all levels
  • Local and state government

Measuring impact of projects and ideas

  • Sharing what did and didn't work
  • Assessing the impact

There seem to be a lot of projects, ideas and little innovations happening around the centre, but I havent seen too much focus on sharing what has and hasnt worked, oustide of staff meetings or centre wide emails.

How do we refine what has been tried and worked, how do improve something that might not have worked?

Sharing our successes

Futhermore, there seem to be a lot of success stories, whether that be successful outcomes for clients or work done outside the centre, from what I have seen these stories help reinforce the importance of the NJC and a community based model
