Why work with government?

  • Every year there is some truly amazing progress being made in digital technology, but typically government agencies tend to be slow moving, slightly backwards organisations that try to tackle all the big problems at once.
  • This can leave government out of touch with the real needs of the public and the communities they work with
  • A quick google search for government IT fails and you can come across some major examples

UK Electronic Health records project

  • 9 year project
  • Cost (2013 estimate): $16 Billion
  • Reasons for failure: too large, too ambitious, too many changing requirements


Source: Colossal failed government IT projects

In 2011, U.K. government officials scrapped a massive 9-year, $16 billion project to create a unified electronic health records system for British citizens.

Reasons for failure: too large, too ambitious, too many changing requirements

LMBR - Dept. Education (NSW)

  • 8 years in the making
  • $531 million pilot Learning Management and Business Reform
  • Thousands of TAFE students left unable to enrol because of system glitches


Source: Sydney Morning Herald, ITNews
  • Started in 2005 and designed to manage human resources, finance, student information and payrolls
  • $400 Million was budgeted, the rollout has not been completed and its already sitting well over $500 million with some estimates putting it closer to $1 billion for completion
  • Its been estimated the same money could have gone to build 20 new schools
  • The NSW Auditors-general cited diverse business requirements and complex commercial arrangements as key factors and have recommend longer projects be capped at 3 year and anything longer be broken down to reviewable chunks

CourtBot

  • Developed by the Atlanta CfA fellows
  • In Atlanta, when you receive a traffic infringement, no matter how minor, the simplest way to resolve it is to go to court and settle the matter. This results in people taking the day off work, only to stand in line waiting to get in.
  • So what courtbot offers, is on the spot information about traffic citations
  • Text your citation number, receive information on how to resolve your infringment

    • CourtBot can provide information on:

    • If a case can be paid immediately, the app offers a phone number and link to begin payment.
    • If a case requires a court appearance, the app allows users to sign up for reminders, served 24 hours in advance of the case.
    • If a case isn't in the system (usually because it takes two weeks for paper citations to be put into the computer), the app allows users to get information when it becomes available. The app continues checking each day for up to 16 days and sends the case information when found (or an apology if not).
    • Steps to take to challenge the citation

Neighbourhood Justice Centre

Doing justice locally and finding lasting solutions that strengthen the City of Yarra
  • Prevent and reduce criminal and other harmful behaviour in the Yarra community
  • Increase confidence in, and access to the justice system for the Yarra communities
  • Strengthen the Neighbourhood Justice Centre Community justice model and facilitate the transfer of its practices to other courts and communities
  • Twitter: @NJC_Collingwood
  • More info: www.neighbourhoodjustice.vic.gov.au
  • So i'm working with the Neighbourhood Justice Centre in Collingwood,
  • The justice centre is australias only community court
  • The centre focuses on utilising wholistic, therapeutic and restorative approaches to justice.
  • Where possible, the centre looks to seek community based alternatives to criminal prosecution, and are committed to helping address the underlying issues of crime.

So what are we doing with NJC

  • Research
  • Prototype
  • Refine
  • Connecting
  • I am engaging with the NJC in a process of research driven prototyping
  • I have been based there for ~3months so far focusing on research, this has involved extensive shadowing of staff, sitting in on interactions with clients and other providers and mapping processes
  • Some of the common themes that have come from my research are issues around interactions with clients of the centre, communicating and sharing knowledge, ideas and new findings,
  • The next steps have involved engaging staff to drive some possible prototypes we can attack based on my research findings as well as mixing in some of my ideas
  • We will spend a couple of months trialing, testing and reviewing the prototypes to see which have worked, what hasnt worked and what could be improved
  • Following that, we will focus on the prototypes that show the most promise for the NJC and spend a few more months refining them
  • As well as the above, we are also helping to connect the NJC with relevant skilled businesses and individuals in their locality, with the skills and passion to help the NJC continue its work
